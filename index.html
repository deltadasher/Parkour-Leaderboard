Meta Category Rulebook

Competitive rank ladder: Unranked → Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond → Master → Elite. Every player currently placed in a ranked-category top 50 is Elite. Time Trials is a score board and does not receive competitive ranks.
Overall Review Score (100 points): 85 points from the average Review Score across a player's ranked-category placements; up to 10 points for category breadth (2.5 per ranked board); and up to 5 points for evidence-qualified per-item depth (1 per item). Time Trials is excluded because it uses a separate in-game score scale.
Per-item Review Score (100 points): usage and mechanical proficiency 30; role-specific gap impact 25; tactical application 15; routing and integration 15; adaptability 10; competitive results 5. One action is credited once under its primary purpose, while its measured distance effect may also count toward gap impact. Item lists contain only players with reviewable current-season use; category placement alone does not qualify a player, and lists are never padded to 50.

Measured set

Three rounds as runner and three as catcher. End gaps are averaged separately for each role; full evidence and meaningful gear usage are required.

Review score · 100

Gear usage/proficiency 25 · integration 15 · runner gap 20 · catcher follow 20 · adaptability 10 · results 10.

Published metric

Review Score is the provisional 0–100 performance summary shown here. Exact in-game Ranked Ratings have not yet been published and are not displayed.

1.Meta Category Definition

The Meta category ranks players based on their combined proficiency with the following gears:

  • Wingsuit
  • Aero Punch
  • Thrust Capacitor
  • Springhook

The category measures complete gear mastery, not merely whether the player equipped or activated all four gears.

A strong Meta player must demonstrate:

  • individual proficiency with every gear;
  • effective combinations between gears;
  • meaningful impact during matches;
  • adaptability across opponents and maps;
  • consistent ranked performance.

2.Leaderboard Format

The Meta leaderboard contains a maximum of 50 ranked players. Players are assigned one of four grades:

  • S Tier: exceptional, proven mastery;
  • A Tier: elite and highly consistent;
  • B Tier: strong and reliable;
  • C Tier: qualified and competent.

Players who do not meet the minimum qualification rules are not placed on the leaderboard. Placement within a tier should still be ordered numerically. Example:

1. PlayerName — S
2. PlayerName — S
3. PlayerName — A

3.Minimum Submission Requirements

A counted evaluation set must give each player both roles and include:

  • three complete rounds as runner;
  • three complete rounds as catcher;
  • the end-of-round gap in metres for all six rounds;
  • separate three-round runner and catcher averages;
  • at least 3 meaningful uses of each required gear;
  • at least 3 different maps or map archetypes, when available;
  • complete and reviewable evidence.

The same opponent may supply the six-round set because both players alternate roles. Additional opponents and sets improve confidence and are required for high placements under section 9.

The required gears must remain available throughout the submitted session.

A player may use other mechanics or non-category equipment only if current ranked rules allow them. Their score must still be based specifically on the four Meta gears.

4.Three-Use Rule

Each Meta gear must be used at least three meaningful times during the submitted session. A meaningful use must contribute to at least one of the following:

  • movement or route completion;
  • gaining or maintaining position;
  • escaping an opponent;
  • pressuring an opponent;
  • winning an engagement;
  • securing an objective;
  • forcing an opponent to react or spend resources;
  • recovering from a mistake or dangerous position;
  • extending or completing a gear combination.

The following do not count:

  • activating a gear into empty space without purpose;
  • repeated spam with no tactical effect;
  • intentionally wasting charges to satisfy the requirement;
  • using the gear after the result of the match is already decided;
  • staged uses performed with a cooperating opponent.

Reviewers may reject individual uses that are clearly artificial.

5.Gear Balance Requirement

Meta is a combined-mastery category. A player may not qualify by relying almost entirely on one gear while using the other three only to satisfy the minimum requirement.

No single gear should account for more than approximately 55% of credited Meta actions. Reviewers may allow reasonable exceptions when:

  • the map strongly favors a particular gear;
  • the opponent repeatedly forces a particular response;
  • the player still demonstrates credible proficiency with every other gear.

Every gear must meet the minimum proficiency floor for the player's assigned tier.

6.Scoring System

Each submission is scored out of 100 points. Scores may use hundredths. Reviewers must retain the component worksheet and the six raw gap measurements; close placements may differ by only 0.01 when the evidence supports it.

6.1 Gear Usage and Proficiency — 25 points

All required gears are reviewed for meaningful usage, mechanical consistency, timing, precision, advanced applications, tactical appropriateness, failure frequency, and the distance gained or lost after use. A failed gear activation that materially collapses a runner gap or prevents a catcher from closing distance must affect this component and the applicable gap component.

A player with a major weakness in one gear should not receive a high overall Meta grade.

6.2 Gear Integration — 15 points

Measures how effectively the player combines gears: successful two-gear combinations; three-gear sequences; variety of combinations; conversion from movement into pressure or objectives; appropriate gear transitions; improvisation during disrupted sequences.

Merely activating multiple gears in succession does not automatically count as integration — the sequence must produce a recognizable tactical or mechanical benefit.

6.3 Runner Gap Performance — 20 points

Gapping is the runner's end-of-round distance from the catcher. Record three runner gaps and calculate runner average = (gap 1 + gap 2 + gap 3) / 3. Higher is better. A runner should normally finish at least 50 m ahead.

Use runner ratio = runner average / 50. Averages from 0–50 m scale from 0–10 points; averages from 50–200 m scale from 10–20 points; cap the component at 20. For 180 m, 92 m and 325 m, the average is 199 m, the ratio is 3.98, and the baseline is 19.93/20.

6.4 Catcher Follow Performance — 20 points

Following is how closely the catcher stays to the runner. Record the three end gaps from the player's catcher rounds and calculate their average. Lower is better; a catcher should normally keep the average below 50 m.

Use follow ratio = 50 / catcher average and follow points = min(20, 10 × follow ratio). A zero-metre catch receives 20 points. The same 180 m, 92 m and 325 m results average 199 m, but for a catcher that is poor: the ratio is about 0.25 and the baseline is 2.51/20.

6.5 Adaptability — 10 points

Evaluated across different maps; open and enclosed environments; aggressive and defensive opponents; both runner and catcher roles; winning and losing game states; disrupted or countered strategies. A high-scoring player must change their approach rather than repeat one memorized sequence.

6.6 Competitive Results — 10 points

Includes weighted win rate; round or score differential; opponent strength; performance against existing leaderboard players; consistency across the full session. Win rate matters, but it must not override clear mechanical or tactical weaknesses.

7.Opponent Weighting

Matches should be weighted according to opponent quality. Full-value matches should generally be against:

  • players within one ranked division;
  • players within approximately 250 rating;
  • current leaderboard candidates;
  • established ranked competitors.

Reduced-value or rejected matches may include: obvious beginners; placement accounts; suspected smurfs with unverifiable identity; repeatedly farmed opponents; cooperating friends; players intentionally throwing; large rating mismatches.

A player should not earn a high placement primarily by defeating weak opposition.

8.Tier Requirements

C Tier

Must satisfy all minimum submission requirements; demonstrate functional use of all four gears; achieve at least a 50% weighted win rate; use basic gear combinations; show no completely unusable gear; provide valid evidence. C-tier players may still display inconsistency, predictable habits, or substantial mechanical mistakes.

B Tier

Must be competent with every gear; achieve at least a 55% weighted win rate; use multiple combinations consistently; demonstrate useful adaptation; avoid frequent wasted activations; remain effective against credible ranked opponents.

A Tier

Must demonstrate strong mastery of all four gears; achieve at least a 60% weighted win rate; produce frequent high-value combinations; perform consistently across different maps and opponents; show few serious mechanical errors; remain effective when their preferred strategy is countered.

S Tier

Must demonstrate exceptional mastery of all four gears; have no major gear weakness; achieve approximately a 65% or higher weighted win rate; perform against strong opposition; show advanced and varied gear integration; remain consistent across multiple verified sessions; demonstrate strategies, routes, or combinations worthy of top-level play.

S tier should remain limited. A strong session alone should not automatically qualify a player for S tier.

9.Additional Requirements for High Placements

Top 10 Requirements

  • at least 3 complete six-round evaluation sets;
  • at least 3 unique opponents;
  • at least 2 verified sessions on different days;
  • at least one set against a current top-50 player;
  • no gear below A-level proficiency;
  • evidence from more than one day.

S-Tier Requirements

  • at least 2 verified sessions (preferably 3 when evidence is disputed);
  • strong-opponent results;
  • high gear-integration scores;
  • reviewer consensus;
  • no unresolved cheating, boosting, or identity concerns.

10.Evidence Requirements

Accepted evidence may include: a full, uncut session recording; server-side replay; official match logs; tournament VODs; moderator-recorded matches; match logs combined with full clips.

The evidence must clearly show the player's username; current rank and, when available, ranked rating; the complete match sequence; the opponents; match results; gear usage; and any relevant score or objective information.

Preferred evidence is a full-session recording beginning before the first queue and ending after the final result.

11.Invalid Evidence

Submissions may be rejected for: missing matches; edited compilations showing only successful plays; unexplained cuts; hidden usernames; unverifiable accounts; missing results; staged matches; arranged wins; private matches submitted as normal ranked matches; replay speed manipulation; incomplete footage; footage from outdated game versions without disclosure; evidence that prevents reviewers from counting gear uses.

Clips may support a submission, but highlight clips alone are not sufficient.

12.Account and Identity Rules

A player must be ranked under one primary Roblox account. Alternate accounts may only be accepted when ownership is verified; the account is publicly linked to the player; the account is not being used to manipulate matchmaking; and all relevant alternate accounts are disclosed.

Account sharing is prohibited. A leaderboard placement belongs to the verified player and account combination. Name changes should be recorded in the player's profile history.

13.Prohibited Conduct

The following may result in submission rejection, removal, or suspension: cheating; exploiting; macros that provide prohibited automation; account sharing; win trading; boosting; intentionally queueing cooperating players; match fixing; falsified evidence; impersonation; harassment of reviewers or opponents; repeated submission spam; hiding relevant bans or alternate accounts.

Accusations must include evidence. Unsupported public accusations may be removed from the forum.

14.Review Process

Each submission should be reviewed by at least two reviewers. Reviewers should:

  • verify evidence integrity;
  • confirm three runner rounds and three catcher rounds;
  • record all six gaps and verify both role averages and ratios;
  • count meaningful uses for all four gears;
  • evaluate opponent quality;
  • assign category scores;
  • propose a tier and placement;
  • document major strengths and weaknesses.

For S-tier or top-10 placements, at least three reviewers should participate. A reviewer should recuse themselves when they are a close friend of the player; a direct rival with an active dispute; participated in staged testing for the submission; or cannot remain impartial.

16.Re-Evaluation and Inactivity

Placements are not permanent. Players may be re-evaluated when ranked balance changes; gears are significantly reworked; the scoring system changes; stronger evidence is submitted; the player becomes inactive; or the player's recent performance substantially changes.

Suggested inactivity labels:

  • Active: verified ranked activity within 60 days;
  • Inactive: no verified ranked activity for 60–120 days;
  • Archived: no verified ranked activity for more than 120 days.

Inactive players may remain visible but should not prevent active players from receiving recognition. Major gear reworks may require a full leaderboard reset or seasonal archive.

17.Appeals

A player may appeal rejected evidence; incorrect match counts; incorrect gear-use counts; factual reviewer errors; tier assignment; penalties; identity decisions.

An appeal must include the original submission; the disputed decision; the exact claimed error; relevant timestamps or evidence; and the requested correction.

Appeals based only on disagreement or popularity should be rejected. An appeal should be reviewed by moderators who did not make the original decision whenever possible.

25.Core Principle

The Meta leaderboard exists to rank complete mastery of the four-gear system. It must not become:

  • a popularity poll;
  • a raw win-rate board;
  • a gear-activation counter;
  • a farming leaderboard;
  • a highlight-edit competition.

Placements should reflect verified proficiency, meaningful gear integration, competitive impact, adaptability, and performance against credible opponents.

Recounter Category Rulebook

Status: Ratified · Version 1.1. Recounter ranks distance control in both roles while requiring Recounter in the back-gear slot. Any legal left-arm, right-arm, and leg gears are permitted and must be disclosed before review.

RC 1.Format and Objectives

Every match contains three Runner rounds and three Catcher rounds. Runners maximize and preserve distance; Catchers minimize distance, predict routes, maintain pressure, and convert proximity into catches or forced mistakes. Neither role may be omitted or used to conceal a serious weakness in the other.

RC 2.Recounter and Loadout Legality

Recounter must remain equipped for the full evaluated match. A match requires at least three meaningful Recounter uses as Runner and three as Catcher. A credited use must gain or reduce distance, recover position, disrupt a route, extend a chase, escape pressure, force a route change, prevent a catch, or secure a catch. Empty, staged, post-result, or quota-filling uses do not count. Supporting gears are free-choice but exploits and temporarily broken interactions may not carry a high placement.

Back Gear: Recounter
Left Arm: any currently legal gear
Right Arm: any currently legal gear
Leg Gear: any currently legal gear

RC 3.Submission Size

  • Standard: 3 matches, 18 rounds, 3 opponents, 9 rounds per role, at least 3 maps.
  • Top 10: 5 matches, 30 rounds, 5 opponents, 15 rounds per role, including 2 current top-50 opponents.
  • S tier: at least 2 verified sessions, 36 rounds, 6 opponents, multiple days, and strong A/S-level opposition.

No more than two counted matches may use the same opponent in a standard submission. Every match must meet the six-use Recounter minimum and have complete evidence.

RC 4.Scoring · 100 Points

  • Gap Control 45: Runner 22.5 and Catcher 22.5.
  • Role Performance 20: Runner 10 and Catcher 10.
  • Recounter Proficiency 15.
  • Routing and Adaptability 10.
  • Competitive Results 10.

Use the shared runner-gap and catcher-follow formulas in Meta sections 6.3–6.4, then scale each 20-point baseline to 22.5 points: category points = baseline × 22.5 / 20. Intermediate telemetry, maximum/minimum gap, recovery, closing speed, and time in favorable states may adjust a component only when the worksheet records the reason.

RC 5.Measurement and Role Review

Prefer telemetry measured through the round. Otherwise use consistent five-second samples, route transitions, post-gear samples, map landmarks, replay tools, and end gaps. Camera appearance alone is insufficient. Record average, maximum, minimum, and end gap; recovery; conversion; favorable-gap time; Recounter uses; mistakes; role; opponent; map; result; and duration. Qualitative Critical/Pressure/Neutral/Safe/Dominant states are allowed at reduced confidence.

RC 6.Tier Floors

C requires functional gap control and meaningful Recounter use in both roles. B requires consistent distance preservation as Runner and reliable reduction as Catcher. A requires strong recovery, efficient routing, timing, and multi-map results against leaderboard-level opponents. S requires elite control in both roles, top-level Recounter proficiency, strong opposition, and consistency across multiple verified sessions. Exceptional performance in only one role cannot earn S.

RC 7.Placement, Evidence, and Review

Comparison priority is gap control, both-role performance, opponent quality, Recounter proficiency, routing/adaptability, then results. Evidence must show identity, loadout, opponents, roles, all rounds, maps, results, gap information, and Recounter usage. Highlight reels, missing losses, staged matches, hidden identities, unexplained cuts, incomplete role coverage, or footage that prevents gap review are invalid.

Two reviewers are required normally; top-10 and S-tier cases require three. Appeals must name the exact round, measurement, usage count, weighting, tier, or placement error and provide timestamps or telemetry. Major Recounter, supporting-gear, map, or telemetry changes trigger re-evaluation.

RC 8.Core Principle

Recounter ranks how well a player controls distance on both sides of ranked tag while using Recounter. Win rate cannot override substantially stronger gap control against harder opponents.

Gearless Category Rulebook

Status: Ratified · Version 1.1. Gearless measures raw movement, routing, prediction, recovery, and distance control without equipped gear. The standard glove is allowed.

GL 1.Format and Equipment

Every match contains three Runner and three Catcher rounds. Any back, arm, leg, movement, utility, temporary, hidden, or pre-activated player-controlled gear invalidates the affected round. Evidence must visibly confirm empty gear slots before the first counted match.

Back Gear: None
Left Arm Gear: None
Right Arm Gear: None
Leg Gear: None
Glove: Standard glove allowed

Glove timing, accuracy, pressure, defensive use, movement conversion, failures, and punishability may be reviewed, but glove skill cannot replace movement and gap control.

GL 2.Submission Size

  • Standard: 3 matches, 18 rounds, 3 opponents, 9 rounds per role, at least 3 maps.
  • Top 10: 5 matches, 30 rounds, 5 opponents, 15 rounds per role, 2 current top-50 opponents, multiple days.
  • S tier: 2 verified sessions, at least 36 rounds and 6 opponents, with strong A/S-level results.

GL 3.Scoring · 100 Points

  • Gap Control 45: Runner 22.5 and Catcher 22.5.
  • Movement and Routing 25.
  • Role Performance 15: Runner 7.5 and Catcher 7.5.
  • Adaptability and Prediction 10.
  • Competitive Results 5.

Apply the shared 20-point runner and catcher baselines, scaled with category points = baseline × 22.5 / 20. Review route efficiency, momentum, wall movement, cornering, vertical transitions, map knowledge, recovery, collisions, and mechanical consistency independently from the final result.

GL 4.Role and Movement Review

Runners create separation without emergency gear, preserve momentum, avoid predictable loops, and recover after route mistakes. Catchers close the starting gap, cut and predict routes, pressure transitions, deny resets, and turn movement superiority into tags. Survival or a late tag cannot erase poor gap control during the rest of a round.

Each round record includes role, opponent, map, result, duration, average/maximum/minimum/end gap, tag time, movement errors, route notes, and reviewer notes. The same gap thresholds must be used throughout a review cycle.

GL 5.Tier Floors

C requires legal equipment, functional movement, basic gap control, and competence in both roles. B requires reliable movement and respectable distance control. A requires strong multi-map routing, recovery, prediction, and few serious errors against leaderboard opposition. S requires elite unaided movement and gap control in both roles across multiple verified sessions.

GL 6.Placement, Evidence, and Review

Comparison priority is gap control, movement/routing, both-role performance, opponent quality, adaptability/prediction, then results. Evidence must show identity, all rounds and roles, maps, results, gap information, and the visible empty loadout. Any gear use, concealed equipment, missing round, omitted loss, staged result, unexplained cut, or unmeasurable footage is invalid.

Two reviewers are required normally and three for top-10 or S tier. Appeals must identify the disputed round and factual equipment, gap, movement, weighting, tier, or placement error. Movement, glove, map, or telemetry changes may trigger re-evaluation.

GL 7.Core Principle

Gearless ranks raw ranked-tag ability without gear assistance. More wins do not outrank clearly stronger movement and gap control against credible opposition.

Gearless: Gloveless Category Rulebook

Status: Ratified · Version 1.1. This category measures pure core movement, routing, prediction, recovery, map knowledge, and distance control without any equipment assistance.

GG 1.Format and Absolute Equipment Ban

Every match contains three Runner and three Catcher rounds. All gear and glove slots must be empty or disabled. Tags use legal body contact or the game's normal gloveless condition. Any gear, glove, temporary utility item, hidden equipment, pre-round carryover effect, or player-controlled equipment activation invalidates the affected round; repeated violations may invalidate the submission.

Back Gear: None
Left Arm Gear: None
Right Arm Gear: None
Leg Gear: None
Glove: Disabled or unequipped

GG 2.Submission Size

  • Standard: 3 matches, 18 rounds, 3 opponents, 9 rounds per role, at least 3 maps.
  • Top 10: 5 matches, 30 rounds, 5 opponents, 15 rounds per role, 2 current top-50 opponents, multiple days.
  • S tier: 2 verified sessions, at least 36 rounds and 6 opponents, with strong A/S-level results.

GG 3.Scoring · 100 Points

  • Gap Control 50: Runner 25 and Catcher 25.
  • Movement and Routing 30.
  • Prediction and Adaptability 10.
  • Competitive Results 10.

Apply the shared runner and catcher baselines with category points = baseline × 25 / 20. With no rescue equipment, gap control has the greatest weight. Movement review includes acceleration, momentum, walls, corners, vertical transitions, landings, geometry, recovery, route variety, collisions, and hesitation.

GG 4.Role, Tag, and Recovery Review

Runners create and preserve distance through core movement alone. Catchers close distance, predict and cut routes, maintain momentum, deny resets, recover after missed contact, and earn legal body-contact tags. A late tag does not erase a chase spent far behind; a failed tag does not erase otherwise excellent pressure.

Recovery after collisions, missed jumps, denied routes, failed approaches, and momentum loss is explicitly reviewed because no gear or glove can rescue poor positioning. Each round records role, opponent, map, result, duration, average/maximum/minimum/end gap, tag time, movement errors, recovery notes, route notes, and reviewer notes.

GG 5.Tier Floors

C requires legal empty equipment, functional core movement, and competence in both roles. B requires reliable movement, recovery, and respectable gap control. A requires strong momentum, prediction, multi-map execution, and few serious errors against leaderboard players. S requires elite pure movement and distance control in both roles across multiple verified sessions.

GG 6.Placement, Evidence, and Review

Comparison priority is gap control, movement/routing, both-role performance, opponent quality, prediction/adaptability, then results. Evidence must show identity, all rounds and roles, maps, results, gap information, visibly empty slots, and the disabled glove. If glove status is not visible, use inventory/settings evidence, an activation test, or moderator observation.

Missing rounds, omitted losses, cuts, hidden identities, staged matches, gear or glove use, concealed equipment, and footage that prevents gap review are invalid. Two reviewers are required normally and three for top-10 or S tier. Appeals must name the exact round and factual gap, equipment, glove, movement, weighting, tier, or placement error.

GG 7.Relationship to Gearless and Core Principle

Gearless permits the standard glove; Gearless: Gloveless does not. Evidence does not transfer automatically. A gloveless match may support Gearless only when cross-category submission rules permit it and the evidence independently satisfies that category. This leaderboard ranks pure movement and distance control without equipment-assisted mechanics.
